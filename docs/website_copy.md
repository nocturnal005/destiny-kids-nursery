### OUR STORY

**Heading:**
Our Story

**Body:**
Destiny Kids Nursery was founded in 2011 with a single, heartfelt mission: to create a place where every child feels safe, valued, and inspired to learn. What started as a small nursery has grown into two thriving branches serving families across Lambeth and Wandsworth.

From our first days in Battersea to our expansion to Brixton Hill, our commitment has never changed. We believe that every child is destined for greatness, and it's our job to nurture the curiosity, confidence, and creativity that will carry them forward.

Today, we are proud to be Ofsted regulated, with a dedicated team of qualified early years professionals who bring warmth, skill, and genuine passion to everything they do.

**Timeline Nodes:**
- **2011** — Destiny Kids Nursery founded in Battersea
- **2013** — Achieved Ofsted registration and first inspection
- **2015** — Launched our first website and online presence
- **2018** — Expanded services and updated to GDPR compliance
- **2020** — Opened our second branch at 228 Brixton Hill
- **2024** — 13 years of care, hundreds of children supported
- **2026** — Continuing to grow, invest, and innovate for families

### EYFS FRAMEWORK

**Heading:**
The EYFS Framework

**Subtitle:**
Everything we teach is guided by the Early Years Foundation Stage — the UK's framework for children's learning and development from birth to 5.

**Intro:**
The EYFS framework sets the standards that all early years providers must meet to ensure children learn and develop well, and are kept healthy and safe. At Destiny Kids, we map every activity to these seven areas:

**Area 1 — Communication & Language**
Developing listening, attention, understanding, and speaking skills through conversation, songs, stories, and role play.

**Area 2 — Physical Development**
Building gross and fine motor skills through outdoor play, climbing, dancing, drawing, and hands-on activities.

**Area 3 — Personal, Social & Emotional Development**
Helping children build confidence, manage feelings, develop friendships, and understand appropriate behaviour.

**Area 4 — Literacy**
Encouraging early reading through phonics, sharing books, mark-making, and beginning to link sounds to letters.

**Area 5 — Mathematics**
Exploring numbers, shapes, patterns, and problem-solving through play-based activities and real-world experiences.

**Area 6 — Understanding the World**
Helping children make sense of the world around them through exploration, technology, nature, and cultural awareness.

**Area 7 — Expressive Arts & Design**
Fostering creativity through painting, music, dance, imaginative play, and building with different materials.

### BABY ROOM (0 – 2 YEARS)

**Heading:**
Baby Room

**Subtitle:**
A cosy, nurturing space where your baby feels safe, loved, and free to explore.

**Body:**
Our baby room is designed to feel like a home within a home. With a high staff-to-child ratio, personalised routines, and a gentle, responsive approach, your little one will thrive in an environment built around their individual needs.

We focus on sensory development, early communication, and building strong attachments — the foundation for everything that comes next.

**Key Activities:**
- **Sensory Play** — Exploring textures, sounds, lights, and materials that stimulate all five senses
- **Tummy Time & Movement** — Supporting physical development and strength building
- **Music & Rhymes** — Songs, rhythms, and lullabies that develop language and listening skills
- **Treasure Baskets** — Collections of natural and household objects for open-ended exploration
- **One-to-One Interaction** — Quality time with key workers to build secure attachments
- **Soft Play** — Safe, padded environments for crawling, climbing, and physical confidence

**Daily Schedule:**
- 7:00 – 9:00: Arrival, settling in, free play
- 9:00 – 9:30: Morning milk/snack
- 9:30 – 11:00: Sensory play and guided activities
- 11:00 – 11:30: Outdoor time (weather permitting)
- 11:30 – 12:00: Lunch
- 12:00 – 2:30: Nap time
- 2:30 – 3:00: Afternoon snack
- 3:00 – 5:00: Free play, music, stories
- 5:00 – 7:00: Quiet play and collection

---

### TODDLER ROOM (2 – 3 YEARS)

**Heading:**
Toddler Room

**Subtitle:**
Where curious minds come alive through creative play, exploration, and discovery.

**Body:**
Toddlers are natural explorers, and our toddler room is designed to channel that boundless energy and curiosity into meaningful learning. Through a rich mix of creative arts, outdoor adventures, and social play, your child will build confidence, language skills, and a love of discovery.

Every day is different, every activity is purposeful, and every child is encouraged to try, fail, and try again — all in a supportive, fun environment.

**Key Activities:**
- **Creative Arts** — Painting, drawing, playdoh, collage, and crafts that develop fine motor skills and imagination
- **Circle Time** — Songs, stories, show-and-tell, and group discussions that build communication and social skills
- **Outdoor Exploration** — Running, climbing, sand play, water play, and nature walks in our garden
- **Construction & Building** — Blocks, Lego, and building activities that develop spatial awareness and problem-solving
- **Imaginative Play** — Role play corners (kitchen, shop, doctor) that develop language and social understanding
- **Early Literacy** — Introducing letter sounds, mark-making, and a love of books through daily reading

---

### PRE-SCHOOL (3 – 4 YEARS)

**Heading:**
Pre-School

**Subtitle:**
Building confidence, knowledge, and school readiness through the EYFS curriculum.

**Body:**
Our pre-school programme is carefully designed to prepare your child for the transition to primary school. Aligned with the Early Years Foundation Stage framework, we focus on building literacy, numeracy, independence, and social skills — all through engaging, play-based activities that keep learning fun.

Children leave Destiny Kids confident, capable, and ready to shine in their next chapter.

**Key Activities:**
- **Phonics & Early Reading** — Systematic phonics sessions, shared reading, and early writing practice
- **Mathematics** — Number recognition, counting, patterns, shapes, and early problem-solving
- **Science & Discovery** — Simple experiments, nature observation, and exploring cause and effect
- **Independence Skills** — Getting dressed, toileting, tidying up, and making choices
- **Social Development** — Sharing, taking turns, resolving conflicts, and working in groups
- **School Transition** — Visits from reception teachers, practising school routines, and building confidence

### GOVERNMENT FUNDING

**Heading:**
Free Childcare Funding

**Subtitle:**
You may be eligible for government-funded free childcare hours — we'll help you claim them.

**Card 1 — 15 Hours Free**
**Available for:** All 3 – 4-year-olds, and eligible 2-year-olds

**How to apply:** Register via the Childcare Choices website, then share your funding code with us

**What it covers:** Up to 30 hours of free childcare per week during term time
**How to apply:** Check your eligibility, get your code, and we'll apply it to your fees
